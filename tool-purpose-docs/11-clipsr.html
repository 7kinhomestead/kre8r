What It Is

The Short-Form Engine

Every long-form video contains several short-form videos waiting inside it. ClipsΩr finds them. It reads the full transcript of your completed video and identifies the moments with the highest viral potential — the emotional peaks, the counterintuitive reveals, the information-dense explanations, the pattern interrupts that make someone stop mid-scroll and watch again.

For every candidate clip, ClipsΩr writes the hook (the first line that makes someone stop), explains why that moment works for short-form, categorizes the clip type, and ranks it. The creator approves or skips each one. No writing required — the copy is already there when you hit approve.

Approved clips flow directly into PackageΩr with their hooks and context already attached. Every downstream platform caption and email will be anchored to the same angles ClipsΩr identified, so the creative thread stays consistent from the long video all the way to the last distribution email.

How It Works

Step by Step

1
Select Project
The completed footage record and full transcript load automatically. Transcript is the primary input — ClipsΩr works on text, not on video frames.
2
Claude Identifies Viral Moments
Claude reads the full transcript and scans for: emotional peaks, curiosity gaps, counterintuitive statements, high information density, pattern interrupts, and moments with strong opinion or conflict. Each is timestamped against the transcript.
3
Clips Returned with Full Context
Each clip surfaces with: hook text, why it works, clip type (educational / emotional / controversial / how-to), rank (1–10), and start/end timestamps. Ranked by estimated scroll-stop potential.
4
Creator Approves or Skips
One click per clip. "Approve All" button available for speed. Skipped clips stay in DB with status = skipped — they don't disappear. Decisions can be revisited.
5
Approved Clips Stored with Full Context
Each approved clip written to viral_clips table: footage_id, project_id, hook, why_it_works, clip_type, rank, status, start_timestamp, end_timestamp. Caption and hashtag fields reserved for CaptionΩr.
6
Handoff to PackageΩr
Approved clips — with hooks intact — are injected as context into PackageΩr's package generation. The angle and hook stay consistent across every platform downstream.
What It Creates

Data & Outputs

Data & Records
viral_clips rows — footage_id, project_id, hook, why_it_works, clip_type, rank, status, caption, hashtags, start_timestamp, end_timestamp
Approval status per clip — approved / skipped, revisable at any time before PackageΩr runs
Files & Outputs
No files at this stage — clip extraction via ffmpeg happens downstream if needed
Timestamp ranges stored per clip — ready for extraction by any downstream tool that needs the physical segment
Valuable Final Product

What You Walk Away With

A ranked, approved viral clip list with scroll-stopping hooks already written — the entire short-form content plan for the video, generated from the transcript, with zero copy written from scratch. Every approved clip arrives at CaptionΩr pre-loaded with its hook and angle, so caption writing starts at step two instead of step one.
