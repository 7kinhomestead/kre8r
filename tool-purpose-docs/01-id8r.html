What It Is

The Idea Engine

Every video starts with a feeling — a half-formed idea, a comment you saw, something that happened in your world that week. Id8Ωr is where that feeling becomes a direction. It gives you three modes depending on where you're starting from, and it asks the right questions so you're not staring at a blank page.

Shape It is for when you already have something — a rough topic, a hunch, a thing you want to say. Claude helps you sharpen it into a clear angle. Find It is for when you have nothing — Claude hunts for the concept that fits your channel, your audience, and the moment. Deep Dive is for when you have a topic but need to understand it thoroughly — Claude researches across the web before it builds your creative package.

Every session ends the same way regardless of mode: a Vision Brief. That's three title options, three thumbnail concepts, and three hook variants — all shaped around your channel's voice and content angles. You pick a direction, and that direction travels with the project through every tool downstream. Nothing in the rest of the pipeline starts without it.

How It Works

The Workflow

1
Pick a mode: Shape It, Find It, or Deep Dive
Mode selection changes the conversation opening and whether web research runs. A session_id is written to localStorage so the tool can retrieve a saved checkpoint even after a tab close or browser restart.
2
Have a conversation with Claude — each exchange refines the direction
Claude reads from creator-profile.json — voice, content angles, platform data — so recommendations are channel-specific, not generic.
3
For Deep Dive: Claude runs sequential web research across multiple phases
Research phases are intentionally staggered with delays between them to avoid API rate limiting. Each phase builds on the previous. After each phase completes, results are checkpointed to the SQLite DB via db.setCheckpoint() — so a server restart mid-session doesn't lose your progress. Known issue: rate limiting can still occur on large research sets.
4
Claude generates the package: 3 titles, 3 thumbnail concepts, 3 hooks
Generated via callClaude() in src/utils/claude.js using claude-sonnet-4-6. The package is shaped by the content angle (financial, system, rockrich, howto, mistakes, lifestyle, viral) selected or inferred during the conversation.
5
You pick a direction — Vision Brief locked
Selected title, thumbnail, and hook are packaged into the Vision Brief. The chat UI history is persisted in localStorage (survives tab close and browser restart). Research results are checkpointed to SQLite after each phase — if the server restarted mid-session, the tool offers to restore from the saved checkpoint using the session_id stored in localStorage.
6
Vision Brief handed off to PipΩr to formally create the project
Vision Brief content passes via URL parameters or sessionStorage to /pipr.html where the project is written into the SQLite database for the first time.
Known Issue / Planned Redesign
Rate limiting can interrupt the research phase. A redesign is planned: fast concept pass first → creator picks direction → deep research runs only on the chosen angle. This cuts wasted research cycles and keeps momentum.

Valuable Final Product

What You Walk Away With

The Vision Brief — a locked creative direction the whole pipeline runs on.

One chosen title, one thumbnail concept, one hook, and the angle rationale. Everything downstream either reads from this or builds on top of it.

  • WritΩr uses the Vision Brief as context when generating the script — so the voice and angle are baked in from the first word.
  • CaptionΩr and MailΩr use the chosen hook as a starting point for platform copy, keeping the creative thread consistent from idea to distribution.
  • Without a locked Vision Brief you're making creative decisions in every downstream tool. With it, those decisions are already made.
