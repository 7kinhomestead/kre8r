What It Is

The Rough Cut Approval Tool

ReviewΩr is where human editorial judgment meets the AI rough cut. EditΩr made the first pass — chose the clips, set the order. ReviewΩr is where you look at that pass and decide: does this work? Each select gets a verdict. Approve the ones that belong. Skip the ones that don't. Reorder anything out of sequence. When you're done, the rough cut is locked and ready to score.

That's it. One job. ReviewΩr is not a short-form tool — ClipsΩr handles viral clip extraction from the completed video with purpose-built analysis. Keeping social clip decisions in ReviewΩr was the wrong stage, the wrong context, and a distraction from the only question that matters here: does the long-form edit hold together?

After approval, clips are extracted via ffmpeg stream copy — zero quality loss, no re-encode — and the approved sequence hands off to ComposΩr for music scoring.

WHY CUTR ANALYSIS MOVED OUT

CutΩr analysis (social clips, retention cuts, CTA placement, off-script gold) used to live here. It was removed because ClipsΩr does the same work — better, later, in the right context. Once the video is complete and scored, ClipsΩr reads the full finished video and identifies viral moments with full production context. Running that analysis on a rough cut at ReviewΩr stage was premature and redundant. The cuts table and /api/cutor/ routes still exist and are used by ClipsΩr downstream — they were just removed from this UI.

How It Works

Step by Step

1
Load Project — Rough Cut Ready
Select the project. The rough cut sequence from EditΩr loads automatically — every select in order, with beat references, footage links, and any b-roll suggestions from the selection run.
2
Review Each Select
Every clip in the rough cut gets a human verdict: Approve (keep), Skip (remove), or Reorder (drag to correct position). Nothing is locked until the creator says so. This is purely editorial — does this clip belong in this video in this order?
3
Extract Approved Clips
Approved clips extracted via ffmpeg stream copy — no re-encode, zero quality loss. Output goes to public/clips/{project_id}/. Fast. Non-destructive. The rough cut is now locked.
4
Handoff to ComposΩr
The approved rough cut sequence passes to ComposΩr for scene analysis and music scoring. One banner. One next step. No branching decisions at this stage.
