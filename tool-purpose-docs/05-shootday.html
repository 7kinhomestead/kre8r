What It Is

The Shoot Day Command Center

Design reality: Remote location shoots. No reliable wifi. Your camera operator's phone is the production bible.

ShootDay takes everything built in pre-production — the shot list from DirectΩr, the crew brief, the script notes — and compresses it into a mobile-friendly checklist that works completely offline. No wifi required. No laptop on location. Just a QR code, a phone, and a plan.

A location shoot is not a studio. The environment doesn't cooperate. Wind picks up, light changes, something unexpected enters frame. ShootDay gives the crew a single source of truth they can carry in their pocket: every shot, in order, with notes, checkable as they go. Nothing falls through the gaps between planning and execution.

The QR code package your camera operator scans is a static HTML bundle — no server dependency. It works on their phone even with zero signal in a remote location. Notes can be added per shot in real time, capturing moments like "good take at 2:14" right where they happened.

How It Works

Step by Step

1
Load Project
Select the project. Shot list from DirectΩr and crew brief load automatically — no copy-paste, no file transfer.
2
Checklist Generated
Each shot list item becomes a checkable task. Shot type, movement, subject, and camera notes all visible inline — your camera operator can see exactly what each beat needs.
3
QR Code Generated
The full offline package — checklist, crew brief, shot notes — is bundled into a self-contained HTML file. A QR code lets your camera operator scan and load it on their phone instantly.
4
On Location: Check Off as You Shoot
Creator and camera operator work through the checklist shot by shot. Items checked off as captured. No paper, no clipboard, no confusion about what's been covered.
5
Notes Per Shot
Any shot can get a note in real time — "nailed at 2:14," "redo: wind noise," "went off-script, keep both." These notes travel with the shot into the post pipeline.
