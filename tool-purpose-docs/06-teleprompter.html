What It Is

Three Devices, One Rig

A real teleprompter costs money, needs a dedicated setup, and doesn't fit in a location shoot bag. TeleprΩmpter replaces the whole rig with three devices the creator already owns — any tablet, phone, or laptop — coordinated over a local network session.

Three roles, three devices. The Display is the screen the creator reads from. The Control device lets someone start, stop, and adjust speed from across the set. The Voice device is the creator's phone — they talk, the mic picks up speech pace, and the script scrolls at the speed they're actually speaking. Hands completely free. Eyes on camera.

A session code locks the connection so a random device on the same network can't accidentally take control. Everything is ephemeral — no data written to the database. When the take is done, the session is done.

How It Works

Step by Step

1
Open on Display Device
Creator opens TeleprΩmpter on the main screen. Script loads from the active project. Session is created with a unique code.
2
QR Codes Appear
Setup screen shows two QR codes — one for the Control device, one for the Voice device. Each deep-links with the correct ?mode= parameter already set.
3
Devices Connect
Control device scans and connects immediately. Voice device scans — session code entry required to prevent unauthorized mic connections from other devices on the network.
4
Voice Drives Scroll
When the creator speaks, their phone's mic detects speech rate. The display scrolls to match their actual delivery pace — not a preset speed. Natural delivery, no adjustment needed.
5
Control Manages Playback
Your camera operator (or any crew member) can start, pause, or adjust speed override from the Control device — useful for retakes, pauses between beats, or if the voice detection needs a nudge.
6
Session Ends, No Data Written
The session is entirely ephemeral. No footage data, no session logs persisted to the database. Content goes to camera — then camera footage flows into VaultΩr after the shoot.
