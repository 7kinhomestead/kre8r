What It Is

Beat Map to Shot List

DirectΩr loads the PipΩr beat map and surfaces it as a formatted shot list. Each beat becomes a shot entry — named, sequenced, and displayed in a format your camera operator can read at a glance on location. It's the first place the story structure becomes a physical production plan.

It also generates a crew brief from those same beats — a short document covering shoot goals, key moments to capture, and the tone of the day. The brief comes from the beat structure, not from a deep script read. That's honest about what it is: a shoot orientation, not a cinematography directive.

DirectΩr and ShootDay share the same beat-map backbone. DirectΩr formats it for planning; ShootDay turns it into an interactive day-of checklist with offline QR access for your camera operator's phone.

How It Works

Step by Step

1
Load Project
Select the active project. DirectΩr fetches the beat map from the DB via /api/shootday/{id}/beats — the same beats PipΩr created and WritΩr built around.
2
Beats Displayed as Shot List
Each beat from the beat map is rendered as a shot entry — beat name, emotional function, and position in the story structure. The list is formatted for your camera operator to read at a glance.
3
Crew Brief Generated
A crew brief is generated via /api/shootday/crew-brief/{id} — Claude summarizes shoot goals, key moments to capture, and tone from the beat map. Stored in the project record. Printable from the UI.
4
QR Package Generated for Camera Operator's Phone
An offline-accessible package is created with a QR code your camera operator can scan on set — the shot list and brief are available without a network connection.
5
Passed to ShootDay
ShootDay reads the same beat/shot list directly. No export step. The list becomes the backbone of the interactive day-of checklist.

V2.0 — Planned Upgrade
AI Shot Direction Per Beat

Today DirectΩr surfaces the beat map as a shot list — it does not yet read the WritΩr script and generate camera-specific instructions per shot. V2.0 will add that layer: Claude reads the approved script, then generates angle, movement, lens choice, and coverage notes for every beat. The shot list stops being a structured summary and becomes a true cinematography brief — the camera operator's on-set director in their pocket.
