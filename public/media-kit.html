01 · THE STORY

STARTED WITH
$300.

Not a retreat from a bad career. Not a lifestyle experiment. A conviction, two babies, and a 22-foot trailer.

Jason grew up in California watching his father — an electrician — build things with his hands and get paid for what he could do, not what he had. That part stuck. The path home didn't include land.

He moved to rural high desert with his wife Cari, two babies, $300, and a 22-foot trailer. No backup plan. No land. No mentor who had done this before. The trailer was home base while they figured the rest out.

"I treated it like a trade, not a dream. Trades have steps. Dreams have vibes."

The first property came through a deal most buyers would have walked away from. The seller needed out fast. Jason had no cash — but he had time, skills, and a particular kind of stubbornness. He built a bathroom for $200 using materials he sourced and bartered. He wired his own solar array instead of paying the $20,000 installer quote — total cost: $4,500. He moved rocks by hand, laid pipe, poured concrete, and built a shop where he now teaches thousands of others to do the same.

Fourteen years later: five kids. A full off-grid homestead. A total lifetime spend of $45,000 — land, house, solar, and shop all included. Monthly bills: $212.

02 · THE PHILOSOPHY

WHAT ROCK RICH MEANS.

The name started as a joke. The property was basically just rocks. It became something more.

THE ORIGINAL ASSETS · "WE'RE ROCK RICH"
ROCK RICH
"Rock Rich is getting everything you want out of life with very little. It's not about being broke. It's about realizing that most of what the world calls a problem, you can solve with your hands."

When Jason first broke ground, the land was mostly rocks. Dense, immovable, inconvenient rocks — the kind that make conventional agriculture impossible and most buyers walk away. He and Cari made a joke about it: "At least we're Rock Rich." And the joke became the framework.

Rock Rich isn't poverty mindset. It's the opposite — it's the recognition that resourcefulness is a superpower most people never develop because they keep trying to solve problems with money before they've tried solving them with skill, time, and creativity.

The Rock Rich Community applies that framework to land ownership, off-grid living, and financial independence. It teaches the numbers, the legal moves, and the sequencing that separates people who dream about land from people who stand on it. The community reached 500+ members in its first three weeks.

06 · THE AUDIENCE

WHO SHOWS UP.

Written from Jason's perspective — because these are people he made a commitment to, not a demographic to sell to.

The people who show up for 7 Kin Homestead aren't looking for a lifestyle blog. They've had enough inspiration. They're looking for proof.

They work. Most of them are somewhere in the paycheck-to-paycheck stretch — not because they made bad choices, but because the system they were handed was designed that way. They grew up watching land ownership, financial independence, and a life built on their own terms get pushed further out of reach with every passing year. They've been told it's not realistic. That it's for other people.

They find 7 Kin because Jason shows his receipts. Every line-item. Every mistake. The actual cost of the solar array, the actual process of the land deal, the actual number on the bathroom tile. They stay because nothing Jason says requires them to first become someone they're not.
